# Master Business Recommendations

## Priority Matrix (Impact vs. Effort)

### High Impact, Low Effort
- **Revise SLA Estimates:** Immediately pad standard delivery timelines presented to buyers in historically delayed states. This directly eliminates 1-star review generation.
- **Credit Card Gateway Redundancy:** Ensure up-time SLAs for the primary payment endpoints, as credit cards hold structural dominance over platform GMV.

### High Impact, High Effort
- **Retention Mechanics Overhaul:** Launch a comprehensive loyalty, retargeting, or immediate follow-up discount infrastructure aimed specifically at converting 1-time buyers into 2-time buyers. 
- **Underperforming Vendor Pruning:** Develop and enforce strict quality thresholds for sellers pushing the lowest reviewed product categories.

### Low Impact, Low Effort
- **Boleto/Voucher Friction Review:** Analyze the user-flow for ticket-based payments to ensure notifications push the buyer successfully to completion before the cart expires.

## Key Risks Identified
- Retention is structurally poor; Olist operates more as a one-stop conduit than a destination marketplace. High CAC will kill margin here.
- Satisfaction is uniquely and dangerously tied to logistical routing lines outside of direct Olist operational control.

## Required Data Follow-ups
- Need specific cost-of-acquisition (CAC) data joined against lifetime value metrics.
- Require deeper granular zip-code level mapping to pinpoint the exact failure nodes in delayed regions.
