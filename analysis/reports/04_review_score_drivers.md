# Review Score Drivers Report

## Executive Summary
- 5-star ratings vastly outnumber neutral ratings, but 1-star ratings peak highly.
- Satisfaction experiences are deeply bimodal (Love or Hate).
- Delivery SLA hits act as the primary catalyst for 1-star ratings.
- Certain product categories attract disproportionately low review aggregates.
- Quality control varies drastically between product subsets.

## Key Metrics
| Metric | Status / Trend |
| --- | --- |
| Score Distribution | Bimodal |
| Delivery Impact | Severe |

## Findings
`04_score_distribution.png` reinforces the bimodal reality of satisfaction. `04_score_by_delivery.png` objectively maps the extreme penalty delayed unboxing has on the customer's likelihood to issue a 1-star review. Specific subset categories repeatedly yield sub-standard scores regardless of SLA.

## Business Recommendations
1. Delist or flag specific sellers within the recurrently low-scoring product categories.
2. Offer instant appeasement (vouchers) if a delivery enters a delayed status prior to the review prompt.
3. Overhaul the specific low-performing product segments to protect marketplace brand equity.

## Assumptions & Limitations
- Excludes users who chose not to leave a review (survivorship bias).
- Assumes review text correlates correctly with the associated numerical integer.
